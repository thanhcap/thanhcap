Carl Jung – The Mind Behind the Collective Unconscious

About Carl Jung

Carl Gustav Jung (1875–1961) was a Swiss psychiatrist and psychoanalyst who founded analytical psychology. He introduced the concepts of the collective unconscious, archetypes, and psychological types, deeply influencing psychology, philosophy, and art.

Key Achievements

  • Developed the concepts of introversion and extraversion.
  • Founded analytical psychology and the theory of individuation.
  • Influenced modern psychotherapy and personality theory.

Inspiring Quote

"Who looks outside, dreams; who looks inside, awakes."
